About Skotos

Q: What is Skotos?

A: Skotos is an online gaming channel that offers a wide variety of games for one low subscription price. We specialize in games that center around roleplaying, great stories, and communication with other people from all over the world. Our flagship games, created in-house, are mostly multiplayer prose roleplaying games, but we also offer graphical roleplaying games and strategy games of all durations.

Q: What kinds of games can I play here?

A: Skotos offers multiplayer online games in three main categories: prose roleplaying, graphical roleplaying, and strategy.

Our prose roleplaying games offer the ultimate in socialization and storytelling, because they're bounded only by your imagination. Our flagship games in this category are: Castle Marrach (a mythical Fantasy game set in an ancient Castle), Grendel's Revenge (a monstrous high Fantasy game), Lovecraft Country (a horror game set in 1930s Arkham), and The Eternal City (a Romanesque Fantasy game).

Our strategy games give you the opportunity to outwit your fellows in competitive venues. We currently have one: Galactic Emperor: Hegemony (an empire-building science-fiction strategy game)

Future offerings will continue to expand the size of Skotos' game channel. We currently have six prose roleplaying games in development: Glory of the Nile, Ironclaw Online, Lazarus Sleeping, Mortalis Victus, Pendragon Online, and Regent's Fate. In addition, we're continuing to talk to fellow game designers about bringing their games to Skotos as Associates.

The Our Games page has more extensive information on our current and upcoming projects.

Q: What's a multiplayer prose roleplaying game?

A: Though we offer many games as part of the Skotos community, our flagship games are multiplayer prose roleplaying games, created in-house. (We also offer a StoryBuilder toolkit, allowing our players to create new prose games all their own.) These games are:

Multiplayer. Our games are available to people worldwide via the Internet. We expect our most successful games to support 1000 or more players (at least 50-100 people online at any time). All of our games are social, and thus you'll be able to interact with these other people, either through cooperation, competition, or a combination of both.

Prose. When you interact with other players, it is through a screen similar to a typical chat room window. Setting, objects, and other people are presented to you as text. Anything that is said, or any action performed, also appears as text. (However, prose is not the entire story, because it is supported by a frame of graphics. Important commands can be accessed by graphical buttons; a map helps to portray your surroundings; in games like Grendel's Revenge important informational pages may be entirely graphical.)

Roleplaying Game. At the heart of any roleplaying experience is the ability to become somebody else. You assume the role of a person with a name, a personality, and a certain set of skills and behaviors — and you pretend to be that person. You immerse yourself in that role, acting as that person would act and saying what that person would say.

Many people enjoy role-playing for the opportunity to play someone completely unlike themselves — someone stronger, or wiser, or more deceptive. Others prefer to play Roles very much like themselves, in order to experience how they might behave in extraordinary circumstances.

Q: What's different or special about Skotos?

A: Skotos is concentrating on creating a community. You'll get to enjoy an entire channel of online games for one fee and will be able to share that experience with thousands of others. Skotos is also concentrating on empowering players and allowing them to become designers. We have dozens of external StoryBuilders working on seven different up-and-coming games for the Skotos community, most of which are still accepting applications for additional team members. We've also given players the opportunity to decide upon the direction of Castle Marrach through our StoryTelling program; Castle Marrach has been in the hands of its players since mid-2001.

Technical Issues

Q: What are the system requirements to play Skotos' games?

A: This is, not surprisingly, a complex question because many of our games work through browsers, which can vary from system to system. Some specific game categories have specific requirements, as listed below, but generally the following requirements apply:

  • There are no notable CPU requirements (technically, some of our graphical games require a Pentium, but mostly you just need a solid web browser).
  • we recommend at least an 800x600 display.
  • We highly recommend Firefox as your browser; all of our browser games except Grendel's Revenge are believed to work on all platforms under Firefox.
  • Internet Explorer is another possible browser on the Windows platform.
  • Generally our games will work if you have not modified your browser's default security configuration. To be precise: you must have cookies turned on; you must allow activeX controls (in Windows/IE) or java (in other browsers); and you must allow pop-up windows.
  • Firewalls, network filtering, or cache servers may cause some problems accessing some games.

The following notes outline specific requirements for different game genres:

Prose Roleplaying. We suggest IE on Windows. Firefox must be used on Macs. We do not currently support Netscape 6, Opera, or Safari due to various problems with those browsers.

Q: What do I do if I'm having lag problems?

A: Lag is generally caused by some computer on the Internet getting overloaded. This can be a machine owned by your ISP, a machine owned by our ISP, or some machine that happens to be routing packets between the two of us. Routing paths are different for different people, and thus lag will usually appear different for different folks.

It's generally helpful to report repetitive lag to us. If you're using a Windows machine you can go to a MS-DOS shell and type "tracert www.skotos.net". This must be done precisely when you are experiencing the lag, and it's helpful to do it a few times, as most lag is intermittent. When you've done that, send the results to ce@skotos.net.

We may or may not be able to offer a solution. If there's a problem with our machine or our ISP we'll be able to fix it. If it's a problem with your ISP, we'll be able to identify the problem precisely so that you can discuss it with them. However, if it's an arbitrary machine on the Internet which happens to be routing packets slowly, it usually takes longer to resolve the problem, because that machine is owned by a company that neither we nor you have contracts with. We can only identify the problem to them and hope they'll resolve it.

In general, lag is a problem everywhere on the Internet. You might feel it a little more at Skotos because you're engaging in more interactive back-and-forth than you would when surfing web pages. However, we also try to work that much harder to reduce lag for our customers in the situations where we actually have control over it.

Q: How is my monthly, quarterly, or yearly billing cycle calculated?

Your account will be disabled if your billing period ends without a new payment. However, we'll save your account and characters for at least a six-month grace period, so that you'll be able to come back and continue playing your old characters if you renew your account within that period.

Our Technology

Q: What technology does Skotos use?

A: The in-house Skotos games (Castle Marrach, Lovecraft Country, Mortalis Victus, and several upcoming projects) are built on DGD, a robust server platform that has been used for numerous chat services, including the popular Yahoo! chat.

We've build a brand-new MUD library on top of DGD that includes some features that were impossible ten years ago, when the last generation of text-dominant games was being written. These features include: an expansive parser featuring more than 1,200 verbs, 2,500 adverbs, and most pronouns and prepositions; a proximity system that creates physical relationships between people and things; layered levels of detail in descriptions; and persistent worlds where players can have real and permanent effects.

Q: Will you be using the same engine for all your games?

A: All of the roleplaying games created at Skotos Tech will be built on DGD, will run using the StoryBuilder Server, and will be editable with the same StoryBuilder Toolkit. However, that's not to say that all of our games will be the same. Different games will use different World libraries which feature specific systems appropriate to the game — such as dueling for Castle Marrach, insanity for Lovecraft Country, and voting for Galactic Emperor: Succession. World libraries will also be available to external StoryBuilders, allowing them to access systems appropriate for the games that they're creating.

Grendel's Revenge and The Eternal City were both built by Worlds Apart on their own Cold-based game engine. Each of our graphical and strategy games as their own core game engine. We may license other games in the future that do not use the DGD engine.

Q: How many players can be on your server at one time?

A: Thousands. DGD is very stable. Every once in a while we hit a plateau, but there is still lots of room for optimization and we haven't hit the upper limit yet.
