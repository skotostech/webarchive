RPGnet Announces Expert Tuesdays

BERKELEY, CA, January 3, 2007 — This month RPGnet kicks off a new feature called "Expert Tuesdays". On the first four Tuesdays of every month, RPGnet is running columns written by experts in various fields detailing how their fields of expertise can apply to roleplaying games.

This month RPGnet kicks off a new feature called "Expert Tuesdays". On the first four Tuesdays of every month, RPGnet is running columns written by experts in various fields detailing how their fields of expertise can apply to roleplaying games.

Each month week #1 begins things with Clerical Error by Thomas Kolar. A student of comparative religion, Kolar explains how religions can work in fantasy games.

Week #2 continues with Legal Injunction by Alex Helm. Helm is an aspiring lawyer and will be looking at the role of the law in real life and in RPGs.

Week #3 moves into the sciences with The Vegetative State of Your Roleplaying by Jennifer Schoonover. Schoonover explores botany, climates, and plants--from herbs to poisons.

Week #4 concludes with Medical Musings by Aesclupius, a medical doctor. His articles provide realistic background for gamemasters to use--from trauma and mental illness to death.

These expert columns join a large library of over 1500 game-related articles available at RPGnet. There are currently 15 active columns at RPGnet, from "A Brief History of Game" to "The Vegetative State of Your Roleplaying", and there is a new article running most weekdays.

All 15 columns are available in the RPGnet Columns section, or via RSS.

ABOUT RPGNET

RPGnet (http://www.rpg.net), the largest indie RPG site on the 'net, has been running since 1996. It features a huge and vibrant forum community as well as daily posts of reviews and articles. RPGnet is a subsidiary of Skotos Tech.

ABOUT SKOTOS

Founded in 1999, Berkeley, California-based Skotos Tech Inc. (http://www.skotos.net) is a developer and publisher of multiplayer games. Its games all center around socialization and new Internet-based communities. Games currently published by Skotos include Castle Marrach, The Eternal City, Ironclaw Online, The Lazarus Project, Lovecraft Country: Arkham by Night, and Monsters! Monsters!: Grendel's Revenge.
