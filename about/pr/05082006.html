RPGnet Forums Go Live (Action)

BERKELEY, CA, May 8, 2006 — Following shortly on the heels of its recent, successful launch of an Actual Play forum, RPGnet has again extended its roleplaying discussion area, this time by creating a new LARP forum at forum.rpg.net.

RPGnet's new LARP forum provides a centralized location to discuss all sorts of Live Action Roleplaying games, from boffer play to Vampire: The Requiem LARPs. Though the forum is newly created, there are already over a dozen active threads, covering the gaming spectrum from fun LARP ideas to specific gamemastering suggestions, from live-action wish-lists to system-specific discussions of Exalted, Fading Suns, Nobilis, and Vampire LARPs.

The RPGnet forums are already the most active indie roleplaying forums on the 'net. With over 5.6 million threads and 8,500 unique visitors viewing the forums every day, the RPGnet forums represent a huge online gaming community.

ABOUT RPGNET

RPGnet (http://www.rpg.net), the largest indie RPG site on the 'net, has been running since 1996. It features a huge and vibrant forum community as well as daily posts of reviews and articles. RPGnet is a subsidiary of Skotos Tech.

ABOUT SKOTOS

Founded in 1999, Berkeley, California-based Skotos Tech Inc. (http://www.skotos.net) is a developer and publisher of multiplayer games. Its games all center around socialization and new Internet-based communities. Games currently published by Skotos include Castle Marrach, The Eternal City, Lovecraft Country: Arkham by Night, and Monsters! Monsters!: Grendel's Revenge.
